Supplier & Ordering Guide

Order smart, protect cashflow, keep us stocked.

Why Stock Control Matters

Bar Franco's ordering philosophy.

📊 20% COGS — maximum

We target a maximum of 20% cost of goods on beverage sales. We invest in wages above the industry standard — which means COGS has to work harder to compensate. We can't control how busy we are. We can control what we spend.

💡 A busy week isn't always a profitable one

Revenue gets eaten fast by over-ordering and dead stock. Stock control is one of the few levers we hold directly — so we use it with intention.

📋 Keep the list focused

Every bottle on a shelf is cash out of our account. A tight, well-curated list that sells through cleanly is more profitable than an impressive one that doesn't move. Nothing gets added without good reason.

🖥 Let Loaded Reports guide every order

Budget, stock received, top sellers — it's all there. If something isn't selling, don't reorder just because it's low. If something is flying, make sure we're covered. Data over habit.

How Billing Works

How our suppliers charge us.

Type 1 — Direct Debit

Debited the Following Friday

Peroni/Asahi, Hancocks, Allied Liquor, and Service Foods direct debit the following Friday after you place the order — regardless of what day you order. So an order placed Monday gets debited that Friday. Order earlier in the week so debits land predictably and don't stack.

Type 2 — Invoice

Paid 20th of Following Month

Campari, Red + White, and Casamassima invoice us — paid on the 20th of the following month. We sell the stock before we pay for it — good terms. Don't order at the end of the month — it falls into the next invoice cycle and makes cashflow harder to manage.

📋 All Ordering Goes Through Loaded Reports

Every order, every time — no exceptions.

✅ Standard process

All ordering must be done through Loaded Reports. Every purchase order needs to be raised in the system before (or at the time of) placing the order with the supplier. This keeps our stock records accurate and our accounts reconciled.

⚠️ Emergency or off-system orders

If you ever have to order through a different avenue — a direct call, an emergency, anything outside the normal flow — still create a purchase order in Loaded Reports, but leave it outstanding. Do not receive it in yet. When the stock physically arrives, that's when you receive it into the system. This way our inventory stays accurate and nothing falls through the cracks.
